介绍 Introduction

So you think you are ready for the big stage ? You have read my book and lighting has no mystery for you. Let’s not forget about politics ! This chapter is mainly addressed to junior artists and students as I will describe my experience in the industry.

所以你认为你已经准备好登上大舞台了？你已经读过我的书了，灯光对你来说并不神秘。别忘了政治！​本章主要针对初级艺术家和学生​，我将描述我在这个行业的经验。

I really wrote this chapter thinking about all the things I would have liked someone to tell me before finishing my studies… I can only promise you : no cliché, no politically correct, no double talk. And to be as fair as possible when it comes to describe ​our beautiful industry​.

我写这章时真的考虑了所有我希望有人在我完成学业之前告诉我的事情……我只能向你们保证：不说陈词滥调，不讲政治正确，不说含糊其辞。在描述我们这个美丽的行业时，我会尽可能公平。

你能教照明吗？ Can you teach lighting ?

First I would like to get something out of the way. The Pixel Cinematography course (seen in Chapter 3) actually starts with an introduction by John Kars, quoting DP Steven Poster :

首先我想说清楚一点。像素电影摄影课程（见第 3 章）实际上是从​**John Kars的介绍开始的，引用了摄影指导Steven Poster 的话**：

Oh, absolutely, no. No, you can’t teach lighting. You can’t teach someone how to light.

哦，绝对不行。不，你不能教打光。你不能教别人如何打光。

What ? I have worked with great artists who actually taught me how to light. I clearly remember Jose and Alfonso showing me how to light a character on Planet 51. When I went to Madrid, I knew how to create some lights and do a bit of layering. And that was it !

什么？我曾与伟大的艺术家合作过，他们实际上教我如何打光。 我清楚地记得 Jose 和 Alfonso 向我展示了如何在 Planet 51 上为角色打光。当我去马德里时，我知道如何创造一些灯光并进行一些分层。就是这样！

They showed me how to give shape to a character and create depth. I have used theirs techniques since then on many movies. I completely agree that the best way to learn is to practice. But having some mentors showing you their tricks and sharing their knowledge is very helpful as well.

他们向我展示了如何塑造角色并创造深度。从那时起，我在许多电影中都使用了他们的技巧。我完全同意最好的学习方法是实践。 但有一些导师向你展示他们的技巧并分享他们的知识也很有帮助。

There is no mystery or gift that would magically make you a great lighting artist. Not at all. Otherwise my book would be pointless. And that is actually the same thing with drawing. I had some great teachers who taught me how to draw.

没有什么神秘或天赋可以让你神奇地成为伟大的灯光艺术家。根本没有。否则我的书就毫无意义了。这实际上和绘画是一样的。我有一些很棒的老师教我如何画画。

Of course the learning curve is linked to your ability to observe and interpret the world. And that is very personal. But I do believe that you can teach and learn anything. Even good taste !

当然，学习曲线与你观察和解读世界的能力有关。这是非常个人的事情。但我相信你可以教授和学习任何东西。甚至包括好品味！

John Kars : All this course can really do is offer a few guidelines, provide some important things to remember, and hopefully point you in the right direction with a solid footing about where to start.

约翰·卡尔斯 (John Kars)： 本课程实际上可以提供一些指导方针，列出一些需要记住的重要事项，并希望能够为您指明正确的方向，让您有一个坚实的起点。

Fine by me !

我同意！

可能性很多 Plenty of possibilities

I personally chose to specialize in lighting for animated feature films. But CG can lead to many other things ! You may join a structure of 500 people… Or create a collective with three friends !

我个人选择专攻动画电影的灯光。但 CG 可以带来很多其他的东西！你可以加入一个 500 人的组织……或者和三个朋友一起创建一个集体！

There are so many possibilities : commercials, feature films, prints, freelance, VR, video games…that I found really difficult to choose a career at first. Do I want to be a generalist ? Work in commercials ? Should I learn other softwares ? How often should I apply to a company ?

有太多的可能性：广告、故事片、印刷品、自由职业、虚拟现实、视频游戏……一开始我发现很难选择职业。我想成为一名通才吗？从事广告工作？我应该学习其他软件吗？我应该多久向一家公司申请一次？

You have so many options in CG that it can be overwhelming. It actually took me quite some time to find my path. I started as a generalist In Brazil, then came back to France as a Flame assistant. That was just not the right choice for me. I was havinga really hard time with clients and short deadline’s pressure. I was kinda questioning my career.

在 CG 领域，选择太多了，让人不知所措。事实上，我花了很长时间才找到自己的道路。我最初在巴西做通才，后来回到法国做 Flame 助理。那对我来说不是正确的选择。我当时很难与客户打交道，而且时间紧迫，压力很大。我有点怀疑自己的职业生涯了。

Fortunately, my grandfather told me something very useful : “First job is always a failure“. It was such a relief ! My career was not over (yes I had a tendency to overreact a bit) and I may pursue my career in CG after all. So, my first advice is : if you are disappointed about your first job in CG, don’t give up. It may improve later on.

幸运的是，我祖父告诉我一些非常有用的话： “第一份工作总是失败的”。 这真是令人欣慰！我的职业生涯还没有结束（是的，我有点反应过度），我最终可能会继续从事 CG 职业。所以，我的第一个建议是：*如果你对 CG 的第一份工作感到失望，*​不要放弃。 以后可能会有所改善。

一个小产业 A small industry

I would have never believed this when I was in school but feature animation is indeed a very small industry. We all know each other and word spreads out very quickly. You have no idea how many e-mails I receive from HR or supervisors asking me about a fellow lighter.

我在学校时绝对不会相信这一点，但动画长片确实是一个非常小的行业。 我们都彼此认识，消息传得非常快。 你不知道我收到了多少来自人力资源部或主管的电子邮件，询问我关于一位同事的情况。

It is no coincidence that the hiring process of many studios includes three recommendations from former supervisors. Word of mouth is very important in our industry. So my first advice would be : don’t behave like a dick because it will backfire at you.

许多工作室的招聘流程都包括前主管的三份推荐信，这并非巧合。在我们这个行业，口碑非常重要。 所以我的第一个建议是：不要表现得像个混蛋，因为这会适得其反。

My first experience as a lighting artist was on “​Planet 51​” in Madrid, with a crew mostly Spanish. I firmly believed back then that these great artists, who barely spoke English, would stay in Madrid once we completed the project. I was so wrong.

我作为灯光师的第一次经历是在马德里的“ Planet 51 ”项目，当时团队大部分都是西班牙人。当时我坚信，这些几乎不会说英语的伟大艺术家会在我们完成项目后留在马德里。但我错了。

Two years later, I worked again with lots of them on “​Happy Feet 2​” in beautiful Sydney. It is really important to behave like a professional because the people you are currently working with probably know your future colleagues.

两年后，我在美丽的悉尼再次与他们中的许多人合作拍摄了《快乐的大脚 2 》。 ​表现得像个专业人士真的很重要，因为你现在的同事可能认识你未来的同事。

​

Trust me, it is a small industry.

相信我，这是一个小行业。

第一份工作很痛苦 1st job is a bitch

The 1st job is the hardest to get. It took me over a year to enter the film industry. And I actually had to expatriate myself ! The story about my first job is pretty funny.

第一份工作是最难找的。 我花了一年多的时间才进入电影界。而且我实际上不得不移居国外！我的第一份工作的故事相当有趣。

I was at the 2007 Annecy Film Festival and I wanted to greet my teachers from my former school Supinfocom. When I arrived at the stand, Fernando Moro, animation supervisor on Planet 51 was asking about students who had particular interest in animation. I told him I was actually more interested by lighting. Which was fine because Ilion “was actually hiring lighting artists as well” he replied. Fernando did not speak English and I did not speak Spanish.We succeeded in communicating by speaking… Portuguese ! Two weeks later, I had an interview with Barbara Meyers and two months later I was landing in Madrid.

2007 年，我参加了安纳西电影节，想向我以前就读的Supinfocom学校的老师们问好。当我到达展台时， Planet 51 的动画主管Fernando Moro正在询问哪些学生对动画特别感兴趣。我告诉他，其实我对灯光更感兴趣。这很好，因为Ilion “实际上也在招聘灯光师”，他回答道。Fernando 不会说英语，我不会说西班牙语。我们成功地通过葡萄牙语进行了交流！ 两周后，我接受了 Barbara Meyers 的面试，两个月后，我抵达了马德里。

I had learned Portuguese in Brazil, my first job.

我的第一份工作是在巴西学习葡萄牙语的。

I know it is quite a coincidence but you never know how things are going to turn out. You make your own luck.

我知道这很巧合，但你永远不知道事情会如何发展。 运气是靠自己创造的。

要求 ？ Requirements ?

Here is another example : on my way to the 30th anniversary of ​*Supinfocom​, I was in the train casually listening to students and junior artists talking about applying to some companies. One comment I overheard (they were actually pretty loud) was “​I cannot apply there, they are asking for +2 years of experience*​. I wish I could.”

还有一个例子：在去参加*Supinfocom* 30 周年庆典的路上，我在火车上随意听着学生和初级艺术家谈论申请一些公司。我听到的一条评论（他们的声音其实很大）是“​我不能申请那里，他们要求有 2 年以上的工作经验​。我希望我能申请。”

My advice would be : DO IT. When a company puts some requirements online, it is a best case scenario. If your profile does not exactly fit, they will not automatically discard your profile.

我的建议是：去做。 当一家公司在网上发布一些要求时，这是最好的情况。 如果你的个人资料不完全符合要求，他们不会自动丢弃你的个人资料。

It actually happened to a colleague of mine who went to… Pixar ! They were looking for people with three years of experience with Renderman and he only had two ! But he got the job. So, give it a try and you might get lucky.

我的一位同事就曾遇到过这种情况，他去了……皮克斯！他们正在寻找拥有三年 Renderman 使用经验的人，而他只有两年！但他得到了这份工作。 所以，试一试，你可能会幸运。

Fear of failure is our biggest enemy.

对失败的恐惧是我们最大的敌人。

​

I would apply even if I only have 3 or 4 years of experience…

即使我只有 3 或 4 年的经验，我也会申请……

Once you have found your first job in the industry, things will get easier. You will have more contacts, you will hear about companies, projects, where and when to apply. If you are bit social, you will grow a network over the years. And that makes a huge difference !

一旦你在这个行业找到了第一份工作，事情就会变得容易起来。你会有更多的人脉，你会听到关于公司、项目、申请地点和时间的信息。如果你善于社交，那么多年来你就会建立起一个网络。 这会带来巨大的不同！

There is a common belief among junior artists that you should know or practice lots of softwares and render engines. That is so untrue. On the contrary ! I have been trained practically on every show I have worked on. Mostly because studios have their own proprietary tools.

初级艺术家普遍认为，你应该了解或练习大量软件和渲染引擎。这完全是错误的。恰恰相反 ！我几乎在参与的每一个节目中都接受过培训。 主要是因为工作室有自己的专有工具。

Generally supervisors do not care so much about which software you used on your last show. At least in feature films. They mostly care about the quality of your pictures and your artistic and technical skills. Since most studios work in PBR with a path tracer, it is much easier to switch softwares than it used to be. Don’t worry about the software, focus on your artistic qualities.

一般来说，导演并不太关心你在上一部作品中使用了什么软件。至少在故事片中是这样。他们主要关心的是你的画面质量以及你的艺术和技术技能。由于大多数工作室都使用路径追踪器在 PBR 中工作，因此切换软件比以前容易得多。不要担心软件，专注于你的艺术品质。

如何选择制作 How to choose a production

If you are lucky enough, you may face another issue. You may get several offers at the same time. And believe me, it can be a tough choice ! In 2007, I had to choose between going to London or Madrid.

如果你足够幸运，你可能会面临另一个问题。你可能会同时收到几份录取通知。 相信我，这可能是一个艰难的选择！2007 年，我不得不在去伦敦和马德里之间做出选择。

I had been dreaming about London for a long time since it was such a famous VFX place. I didn’t know anything about Madrid nor did I speak Spanish. A very good friend of mine gave me this advice : “​Don’t go to Ilion, they work with 3ds Max. ​” I am glad I did not listen to his advice. Going to Ilion has been one of the best decisions I ever took : it was the beginning of my career in Animation.

我一直梦想着去伦敦，因为它是如此著名的视觉特效之都。我对马德里一无所知，也不会说西班牙语。我的一个非常好的朋友给了我这样的建议：“不要去伊利昂，他们使用 3ds Max。 ”我很高兴我没有听从他的建议。 去伊利昂是我做过的最好的决定之一：这是我动画职业生涯的开始。

Softwares should never be a criteria to choose a production in my opinion. A software is just a tool to make images. What does matter is the quality of the images. So you can train your eye and become a better artist. Of course if I could, I would only work with Guerilla Render. But it must not be my first and only criteria.

我认为软件永远不应该成为选择作品的标准。 软件只是制作图像的工具。重要的是图像的质量。这样你就可以训练你的眼睛，成为一个更好的艺术家。 当然，如果可以的话，我只会使用 Guerilla Render。但这不应该是我的首要和唯一标准。

我的个人标准 My personal criteria

Here is a non-exhaustive list of my own :

以下是我自己的非详尽列表：

  • The project is my number one criteria. If the project does not have a minimum of artistic quality, I find it very difficult to invest myself.
  • 项目是我的首要标准。如果项目没有最低限度的艺术品质，我会很难投资。
  • Colleagues are also very important. Do you know someone who works at this place already ? How does he feel about the project ? Maybe he can give you an hint on how things are going.
  • 同事也很重要。你认识已经在这里工作的人吗？他对这个项目有什么看法？也许他可以给你一些关于事情进展的提示。
  • Interview feedback really matters. How did you feel when talking to your possible future colleagues ? Did you get a good vibe from them ? How did they describe the company or the project ?If something seems off, listen to your guts.
  • 面试反馈真的很重要。当你和你未来可能的同事交谈时，你感觉如何？他们给你留下好印象吗？他们如何描述公司或项目？如果感觉不对劲，听从你的直觉。
  • Salary can also make a big difference. How expensive is the rent, the food or the transportation ? Does the company offer any relocation package ? You may never talk salary with your supervisors during the interview. This generally takes place later with HR.
  • 薪水也会造成很大影响。房租、伙食或交通费有多贵？公司是否提供搬迁补助？面试时，你可能永远不会和主管谈薪水。这通常会在面试后与人力资源部门进行。
  • Company status is key. Does the studio own its product or not ? What is its relationship with the client ? Did it have to bid against others VFX vendors ? Does he have control other artistic decisions ?
  • 公司地位是关键。工作室是否拥有自己的产品？它与客户的关系如何？它是否必须与其他 VFX 供应商竞标？他是否有权控制其他艺术决策？

Regarding the company status, switching from Cinesite (VFX vendor) to On Animation (studio) made a huge difference for me. Some VFX companies, in order to win the bidding of a show, lower their budget too much. If a show would need 25 artists across all departments, they will have to deliver on time with maybe 12 artists, which means a lot of pain and sweat.

关于公司状况，从 Cinesite（视效供应商）转到 On Animation（工作室）对我来说有很大不同。一些视效公司为了赢得节目竞标，会大幅降低预算。如果一个节目需要所有部门的 25 名艺术家，他们就必须按时交付，可能只有 12 名艺术家，这意味着很多痛苦和汗水。

有关这个主题的有趣的视频。

我的采访 My interviews

As a Sequence Supervisor, I am mostly looking for three things :

作为序列主管，我主要关注三件事：

  • Quality has always been a very important factor of decision for me. I kinda need to fall in love with the project I am going to work with.

    质量对我来说一直是一个非常重要的决策因素。我需要爱上我将要从事的项目。

  • Responsibilities is something I am always looking for. I like to be in charge of a team or a department, especially training or managing.

    我一直在寻找责任感。我喜欢负责一个团队或一个部门，尤其是培训或管理。

  • To be busy. I need to be busy. In the past two companies have hired me to do nothing. I would like to avoid as much as I can this kind of situation.

    忙碌。 我需要忙碌。过去有两家公司雇佣我无所事事。我希望尽可能避免这种情况。

Here is a very personal list of questions I ask during my interviews :

以下是我在面试中提出的非常私人的问题列表：

  • How big is my team ? 我的团队有多大？
  • What would my tasks be ? 我的任务是什么？
  • How many leads are there ? 有多少条线索？
  • What am I responsible for ? 我要负责什么？
  • How many people are above me ? 我上面还有多少人？
  • What quality are you aiming at ? 你追求的是什么样的品质？
  • How many people are they in the department ? 该部门有多少人？
  • Who does the compositing of the shots ? 谁负责合成这些镜头？
  • How do you work on a sequence ? 您如何处理一个序列？
  • Which softwares do you use ? 你使用哪些软件？
  • How long is the production ? 生产周期是多久？
  • What is the budget of the movie ? 这部电影的预算是多少？
  • How long are your renders ? 你的渲染有多长？
  • How are dailies organized ? 日报是如何组织的？
  • Can I train my team? Make presentations and demos? 我可以培训我的团队吗？可以做演讲和演示吗？
  • Do have you lighting meetings ? 你们有照明会议吗？
  • Will in be in touch with R&D ? 会与研发部门保持联系吗？

In a department, you need both seniors and juniors. Do not hire on CV or demo reel, but on knowledge and attitude. Seniors will provide the experience and juniors the energy and motivation.

在一个部门里，你既需要资深员工，也需要初级员工。不要只看简历或演示视频，而要看知识和态度。资深员工会提供经验，初级员工则会提供能量和动力。

口口相传 Word of mouth

I would never choose a company based on its reputation. Because “​a big and famous name​” is not enough. Best example that comes to my mind would be Animal Logic. I did two shows over there : Lego Batman and Ninjago.

我绝不会根据声誉来选择公司。因为“​一个大而有名的名字​”是不够的。我能想到的最好的例子是 Animal Logic。我在那里做过两个节目：Lego Batman和Ninjago。

Same crew, same softwares, same building… Two completely different experiences ! And the same happened on Happy Feet 2. My friend Jerome had a blast working in animation, while I was having a rough time in lighting.

同样的团队、同样的软件、同样的建筑……两种截然不同的体验！ 《快乐的大脚 2》也经历了同样的情况。我的朋友杰罗姆在动画方面工作得很开心，而我在灯光方面却遇到了麻烦。

Can a production budget be a criteria ? If you are working on a very low budget, like 10 millions of dollars for a feature film, you will struggle to reach some quality. But the other extreme is not ideal neither : if you have too much money on a show, people will not care about optimization nor efficiency and you may end up with 80 iterations per shot. I personally think ​the average of$70 million budget is comfortable​. You have enough money to reach some quality but not too much, so people have to be smart on their way of working.

制作预算可以作为标准吗？如果你的预算非常低，比如一部故事片的预算只有 1000 万美元，那么你将很难达到一定的质量。但​另一个极端也不理想​：如果你在一部剧集上投入了太多资金，人们将不会关心优化和效率，最终你可能会每拍摄一个镜头进行 80 次迭代。我个人认为​平均 7000 万美元的预算是合适的​。你有足够的钱来达到一定的质量，但不会太多，所以人们必须以聪明的方式工作。

My tuppence.

我的两便士。

厨师太多 Too many cooks

I learned this interesting expression from my lead on Ninjago. When you have too many people giving their opinion on a movie, it can feel like the kitchen is a bit crowded. Nightmare productions generally have the syndrome of vertical organization :

我从 Ninjago 的领导那里学到了这句有趣的话 。 当有太多人对一部电影发表意见时，你会觉得厨房有点拥挤。 噩梦般的制作通常都有垂直组织综合症：

  • An artist shows his shot to his lead and gets some notes.
  • 一位艺术家向他的主角展示了他的作品并获得了一些评论。
  • The lead shows the shot to his supervisor and may get different or contradictory notes.
  • 主角向他的主管展示镜头，可能会得到不同甚至矛盾的反馈。
  • After some iterations, the lighting sup will present the shot to the CG/VFX sups (there may be few of them) and will probably get more notes.
  • 经过几次迭代后，灯光主管会将镜头呈现给 CG/VFX 主管（可能有几位），并且可能会得到更多注释。
  • Eventually the shot will be shown to the director and get approved. Or completely changed. You never really know.
  • 最终镜头会被展示给导演并获得批准。或者完全改变。你永远不知道。

This kind of organization presents two main issues :

这种组织存在两个主要问题：

  1. You may get lots of notes, sometimes contradictory, if you cannot anticipate the director’s vision (if he has one).
  2. 如果你不能预测导演的想法（如果他有的话），你可能会收到很多意见，有时甚至是相互矛盾的。
  3. People get frustrated and do not feel valued which results in a lack of investment and motivation.
  4. 人们感到沮丧，感觉不被重视，导致缺乏投入和动力。

It is also very important to define precisely the roles. A lead lighting role at Illumination can be very different to a lead lighting role in an other company.

明确定义角色也很重要。Illumination 的首席照明角色与其他公司的首席照明角色可能有很大不同。

After the interview, Animal provides a list of very precise tasks and expectations for each role. No bad surprises in this case.

面试后，Animal 会提供一份非常详细的任务清单，以及对每个职位的期望。在这种情况下，没有什么不好的意外。

合理的解决方案 A logical solution

But there is a great solution to that ! For example, at Animal Logic, things were quite different. As as senior, you would work on your shot with a lot of autonomy and in dailies you would show directly to the VFX supervisor or Production Designer.

但有一个很好的解决方案！例如，在 Animal Logic，情况就大不相同。作为资深人员，您可以非常自主地处理您的镜头，并且在每日拍摄中，您可以直接向视觉特效主管或制作设计师展示。

This process was applied on a department level and made our lives much easier. We would have much less steps in the chain of command. This is probably the reason why I felt so fulfilled on Lego Batman.

这一流程在部门层面得到应用，让我们的工作变得轻松很多。我们的指挥链步骤会少很多。这或许就是我制作《乐高蝙蝠侠》时感到如此满足的原因 。

Can you imagine ? A senior artist talking directly to Grant during dailies ? No intermediary. Not only it was more efficient but it also motivated me a lot. I experienced a similar process at On Animation with Rémi Salmon. It is probably cheaper as well for the studio since the shots get approved more smoothly. Win-win.

你能想象吗？一位资深艺术家在每日拍摄期间直接与 Grant 交谈？没有中间人。这不仅效率更高，而且也激励了我很多。 我在 On Animation 和**Rémi Salmon** 一起经历了类似的过程。对于工作室来说，这可能也更便宜，因为镜头审批更顺利。双赢。

**Ben Snow**​: “In the business of looking at images, everyone is an expert and will offer their opinion.”

**本·斯诺**​： “在图像查看行业中，每个人都是专家，都会提供自己的意见。”

So true. People just love to comment, even if they have no idea.

确实如此。人们就是喜欢发表评论，即使他们不知道。

履行合同 Fulfill your contract

I know it can sound stupid. But while you are on a show, having possibly a difficult time, you may have sometimes to juggle with offers from other companies; which may look quite tempting. As one may say : “​Grass is always greener on the other side of the fence. ​”

我知道这听起来很蠢。但是当你参加节目时，可能会遇到困难，有时你可能不得不考虑其他公司的报价；这些报价可能看起来很诱人。正如人们所说的那样：“别人家的草总是更绿。 ”

But if you have signed a contract, I would highly recommend you to fulfill it. When you sign on a project, you are committed to a company and people expect you to respect this agreement.

但是如果你已经签了合同，我强烈建议你履行合同。当你签署一个项目时，你就对一家公司做出了承诺，人们希望你遵守这份协议。

It is in my opinion something not to take slightly. When having a rough time, I often think of this great quote from Churchill : “Never never never never never give up“. There is a tendency in the industry to think that it is okay to leave a project before it is actually delivered.

在我看来，这是一件不容小觑的事情。当遇到困难时，我经常会想起丘吉尔的这句名言： “永不放弃”。 业内有一种倾向，认为在项目真正交付之前放弃是可以的。

That’s actually a big deal. If you are really not enjoying on a project, speak up. Solutions can be found, sometimes in an unexpected way.

这其实是一件大事。 如果你真的不喜欢某个项目，那就说出来。 有时可以找到解决方案，而且方法出乎意料。

生活是一个优先考虑的问题 Life is a question of priorities

We all have different priorities : it may be traveling, working on your own projects, having some stability, working on big movies… And these priorities are personal and you will take important decisions in your life based on them.

我们都有不同的优先事项：可能是旅行、从事自己的项目、保持稳定、拍摄大电影……​这些优先事项都是个人的​，你会根据它们做出生活中的重要决定。

Right after finishing school Bastien Dubois went to Madagascar to direct his short film. It was a bold choice back then ! Two years later, he got an academy award nomination for it in 2011. Same thing with Alexandre Espigares who was working on Happy Feet 2 and had to leave to direct his short film in Luxembourg. He actually won the academy award in 2014 !

巴斯蒂安·杜波依斯一毕业就去了马达加斯加导演他的短片。这在当时是一个大胆的选择！两年后，他凭借这部电影获得了 2011 年奥斯卡奖提名。亚历山大·埃斯皮加雷斯也是如此，他当时正在制作《快乐的大脚 2》，不得不离开去卢森堡导演他的短片。他实际上在 2014 年获得了奥斯卡奖！

My personal choice was to travel, work on big movies and learn from the best artists. And there is a rule I have come up with to know if you are in the right place or if you should change company. I think that you are not wasting your time in a job if :

我个人的选择是旅行、拍大片以及向最优秀的艺术家学习。我总结了一条规则，让你知道你是否在正确的位置或者是否应该换公司。我认为，如果符合以下条件，那么​你在工作上就没有浪费时间​：

  • You learn lots of stuff. Learning is essential in our industry and if you actually learn useful knowledge at your job, stay there.
  • 你可以学到很多东西。 在我们这个行业，学习是必不可少的，如果你在工作中真正学到了有用的知识，那就留在那里。
  • You earn a lot of money. Well money can be useful, right ? To buy a house or a car. So if you are saving some money and you are not bored to death, stay there.
  • 你赚了很多钱。 钱还是有用的，对吧？买房子或买车。所以如果你在存钱，而且不至于无聊透顶，那就呆在那里吧。

Ideally you would have both at the same time but that is very rare. If you have neither of these things, maybe it is time for you to move on. The hard part is leaving a studio where things are going well. To take that risk.

理想情况下，你会同时拥有这两样东西，但这种情况非常罕见。如果你两者都没有，也许是时候继续前进了。 困难的部分是离开一家发展良好的工作室。承担这个风险。

制作效果 The making-of effect

When I was a student, I kinda thought that animation studios were like a big family where anyone could give their opinion, propose stuff to improve the movie and go down the hallway on a scooter. Well friends, here is my biggest truth : ​professional life is NOT a DVD bonus​.

当我还是学生时，我有点觉得动画工作室就像一个大家庭，任何人都可以发表意见，提出改进电影的建议，骑着滑板车在走廊里闲逛。朋友们，这是我最大的真理：​职业生涯不是 DVD 奖励​。

  • NOT Everyone can give their opinion. Basically you just do what you are being told.
  • 并非每个人都可以发表意见。 基本上你只需按照别人说的做就行了。
  • Technical problems are part of the job. You spend lots of time cleaning stuff.
  • 技术问题是工作的一部分。 你要花很多时间清理东西。

I can count on the fingers of ONE hand gigs where I felt fulfilled as an artist. And I will repeat in bold and with emphasis because it is CRITICAL : technical issues are the norm in the industry, especially in lighting where we receive publishes from all departments. So you better get used to it since ​ALL companies face these problems sooner or later​.

作为一名艺术家，我感到满足的演出屈指可数。我会用粗体字重复一遍，因为这很重要： 技术问题是这个行业的常态， 尤其是在灯光方面，我们会收到来自所有部门的发布。所以你最好习惯它，因为​所有公司迟早都会面临这些问题​。

Our job as a lighting artist is not to create lights and make it pretty. Since we are the ones rendering the images of the movie, our responsibility is much bigger. Since we are at the end of the chain, we make sure for every other department that things render correctly.

作为灯光师，我们的工作不是创造灯光并使其变得漂亮。由于我们是渲染电影图像的人，所以我们的责任要大得多。由于我们处于链条的末端，因此我们要确保其他每个部门的渲染都正确无误。

Here is a non-exhaustive list of technical tasks you may have to perform as a lighting TD/artist :

以下是作为灯光技术总监/艺术家可能需要执行的技术任务的非详尽列表：

  • Debug the shot : why is it crashing on the farm ?
  • 调试镜头：​为什么它在农场崩溃了​？
  • Optimize render times : why is it so expensive ?
  • 优化渲染时间：​为什么这么昂贵​？
  • Update assets : are you rendering the latest version ?
  • 追踪问题：为何草不见了？
  • Track issues : why is the grass missing ?
  • 更新资产：您正在渲染最新版本吗？
  • Improve the shot : can you move this object and tweak its shader ?
  • 改善镜头：您可以移动这个物体并调整其着色器吗？

Unless you accept those as a daily normal task, you’ll be very unhappy. There are just too many videos and making-of out there trying to sell us this dream job where everyone works hand-in-hand.This non-sense is actually doing harm to our industry.

除非你接受这些作为日常的正常任务，否则你会非常不开心。有太多的视频和花絮试图向我们推销这份每个人都齐心协力的梦想工作。这种胡说八道实际上正在损害我们的行业。

耐心是关键 Patience is the key

Do NOT rush. If something looks wrong, let’s analyze and try to understand what is going on. These making-of from Pixar and Disney, they really harmed the industry.

不要着急。如果发现有什么不对劲，让我们分析一下 ， 试着了解到底发生了什么。皮克斯和迪士尼的这些幕后花絮确实损害了整个行业。

There is no magic place where everything works perfectly. There are only different places with more or less money and people sweating over their computers.

世界上并不存在一个万事皆完美的神奇地方。 只有资金多或少的地方，以及在电脑前忙碌的人们。

Each production is a bet. You never know how things are going to look like. Even at a big famous studio like Animal Logic ! We did most of Lego Batman in three months ! The first Lego movie was saved by a lighting TD who developed Glimpse on his own time.

每次制作都是一场赌注 。 你永远不知道事情会变成什么样子。即使是在 Animal Logic 这样的著名大工作室！我们在三个月内完成了《乐高蝙蝠侠》的大部分制作！第一部乐高电影被一位灯光总监拯救了，他利用业余时间开发了 Glimpse。

Companies rely on human beings, a big name is not the insurance of a quality production. The software does not give the quality. The artist does.

公司依靠的是人，大牌并不能保证产品质量。软件不能保证产品质量。艺术家才是质量的保证。

I would like to clarify something here : the average production time in lighting is around a year. The longest I have done was Planet 51 (around 21 months) and the shortest was The star (5 months or so).

我想在这里澄清一点：灯光制作的平均时间约为一年。 我制作时间最长的是​Planet 51​（约 21 个月），最短的是​The star​（约 5 个月）。

The three months (mentioned above) of Lego Batman were actually three months of crunch time.

《乐高蝙蝠侠》 的三个月（上面提到的）实际上是三个月的关键时期。

随着时间的推移 Overtime

Crunch time is part of every production. I can’t remember a single show where I did not work a Saturday or extra hours at night. It is completely normal in our industry. Even if you plan everything as best you can, there is always something unexpected along the way. Who knows ? It might be a happy accident that hopefully makes the movie better.

关键时刻是每部作品的一部分。我不记得有哪部电影我没有在周六或晚上加班。这在我们这个行业是完全正常的。 即使你尽你所能地计划好一切，过程中也总会有一些意想不到的事情发生。谁知道呢？这可能是一个美好的意外，希望它能让电影变得更好。

Craig Welsh was really clear about overtime policy : “You must fill your time sheet with the real hours you put in. Otherwise, we will undervalue the cost of the movie production and for our next bid, the client will therefore ask for a lower budget. It will start a vicious circle if you do that.”

克雷格·韦尔什对加班政策非常清楚：“你必须在时间表上填写你实际投入的时间。否则，我们将低估电影制作的成本，因此在下一次投标时，客户将要求降低预算。如果你这样做，就会陷入恶性循环。”

It is not okay not to get paid for overtime. You should at least get paid on a normal rate for any extra hour you put in. Otherwise you are just killing the industry. You are burying your own grave by doing free overtime. Some producers may try to take advantage of you if you are a junior artist. So pay attention.

加班不拿工资是不对的。你至少应该按正常标准为你加班的时间拿工资。否则你就是在毁掉这个行业。免费加班就是在自掘坟墓。 如果你是初级艺术家，一些制片人可能会试图利用你。所以要注意。

A student asked me once what I thought about the Rockstar controversy. I have done 90-hour weeks on Happy Feet 2 and The Star. And all I ask for is to get paid. Don’t ever do free overtime.

有一次，一个学生问我对Rockstar 争议的看法。我曾为《快乐的大脚 2》和《The Star》工作过 90 个小时。我唯一的要求就是获得报酬。永远不要免费加班。

旧约争议 The OT controversy

I got a very interesting conversation about overtime with an artist from London. Main question was : is overtime compulsory in our industry ? I do think that overtime is part of our industry. Movies are an art form and even if you have a clear idea of your script, you never really know how things are going to work out (in public screenings for example).

我和一位来自伦敦的艺术家就加班问题进行了一次非常有趣的对话。主要问题是：在我们这个行业加班是强制性的吗？ 我确实认为加班是我们这个行业的一部分。电影是一种艺术形式，即使你对剧本有清晰的想法，你也永远不知道事情会如何发展（例如在公映时）。

Even if you plan things with lot of care, the creativity and the artistic changes you are going to face during production will cause overtime most of the time. Having said that, I also agree that studios take advantage of this when they should not.

即使你精心计划，在制作过程中你将要面对的创造力和艺术变化也大多数情况下会导致加班。话虽如此，我也同意工作室在不该利用这一点的时候利用了这一点。

Do you do less overtime with a $70 million budget since people are supposed to work smarter ? I would say on paper, yes. Production has less budget so supervisors are forced to find smart solutions. Best example would be Animal Logic I think. I do think their ratio budget/quality is pretty much unbeatable.

鉴于人们应该更聪明地工作，你们是否会在 7000 万美元的预算下减少加班时间？理论上，我会说是的。制作预算较少，因此主管被迫寻找明智的解决方案。我认为最好的例子是 Animal Logic。我确实认为他们的预算/质量比率几乎无与伦比。

But it does not mean you will do less overtime. I did three movies over $200 million budget in my career (​Happy feet 2​, Avatar and ​Apes​) and it was a similar pattern every time : unlimited OT and not much control. Funny thing, the worst OT I ever did were on HF2 and the Star with very different budgets ! So the connection between budget and OT is not necessary logical.

但这并不意味着你的加班时间会减少。在我的职业生涯中，我拍摄了三部预算超过 2 亿美元的电影（​ 《快乐的大脚 2》 ​、 《阿凡达》 和 《猩球崛起》），每次都是类似的模式：无限加班，没有太多的控制。有趣的是，我拍过的最差加班是在《快乐的大脚 2》和《星球大战 5：最后的绝地武士》中，预算差别很大！因此，预算和加班之间的联系不一定合乎逻辑。

补休 Time off in lieu

Sometimes companies will offer you TOIL (Time off in lieu) instead of getting paid overtime. What does this mean ?

有时公司会提供补休（TOIL）来代替加班费。这是什么意思？

Time off in lieu (also known unfortunately as ​TOIL​) is where you agree to give an employee paid time off in lieu of extra hours they have worked (overtime) instead of extra pay.

补休（不幸的是也称为​TOIL​）是指您同意给予员工带薪休假以代替其额外的工作时间（加班），而不是支付额外工资。

Definition from this website.

定义来自于本网站。

I have very little experience with TOIL so please excuse my naivety. Here is what I can tell you : I did some overtime on Lego Batman which gave me the right to more holidays, right ? I actually never got the time to use these holidays during production. So I got a big check when my contract ended. That’s it.

我对 TOIL 的经验很少，所以请原谅我的幼稚。我可以告诉你的是：我在《乐高蝙蝠侠》上加班了一段时间，这让我有权享受更多假期，对吧？实际上，我在生产期间从未有时间使用这些假期。所以当我的合同结束时，我得到了一张大额支票。就是这样。

In my very limited and personal experience TOIL was some money when I left, like some sort of delayed OT. Some people told me to be careful with TOIL since the company could go bankrupt at the end of production and therefore you would lose all your extra days/money… It never happened to me though.

就我个人经验而言，我非常有限，但当我离开时，加班费就是一笔钱， 就像某种延迟加班一样。有人告诉我，加班费要小心，因为公司可能会在生产结束时破产，因此你会失去所有的加班天数/金钱……但我从未遇到过这种情况。

指责游戏 The blame game

I recently discovered this expression and I found it quite interesting. Departments working hand-in-hand is very rare. “​***The blame game***​” is actually quite common. I guess it is just a typical human behavior : something is not working, you get fed up and you blame others. In lighting we would typically blame the surfacing artists. And compositing artists usually blame lighters…

That is something you do not see in many making-of, right ? A very good solution to this is to make ​inter-departmental meetings​. At Animal Logic, we had a one hour presentation every week about various topics : it could be matte-painting, color management or surfacing. I found it very useful to learn about other’s departments challenges as it ​makes your vision broader​. As lighters we are not necessarily aware but Set Surfacing generally faces two kinds of issues :

这是你在很多制作过程中看不到的东西，对吧？一个很好的解决方案是召开​跨部门会议​。在 Animal Logic，我们每周都会进行一小时的演讲，讨论各种主题：可能是绘景、色彩管理或表面处理。我发现了解其他部门的挑战非常有用，因为它​可以让你视野更开阔​。作为灯光师，我们不一定意识到这一点，但场景表面处理通常面临两种问题：

For example some big studios do not care about automatizations. They basically have enough manpower to overcome any task. So they will just keep working manually since they have been doing this for thirty years in a rather successful way.

四处走动 Moving around

I had the chance and privilege to travel during eight years : Brazil, Spain, England, Australia, Scotland, France, Australia, New-Zealand and Canada.

Traveling when you are single is great and easy. I would just pack my suitcase (I like to travel light) and live in a fully furnished studio. You get to meet new people, discover a new culture and learn a new language. When you are in a couple or with family it can be more difficult obviously. So before taking the big leap, here is my advice :

单身时旅行既简单又好玩。我只需要收拾行李（我喜欢轻装旅行）然后住在设施齐全的单间公寓里。你可以结识新朋友，发现新文化，学习新语言。当你和伴侣或家人在一起时，旅行显然会更困难。所以在迈出这一步之前，以下是我的建议：

  • Living in a foreign country is not easy. You’ll have to open a bank account, pay your taxes there, find a place to live. It is not a magical fairyland.

    在国外生活并不容易。 你必须在那里开立银行账户、纳税、找住处。这不是一个神奇的仙境。

  • You can get homesick especially if you are living far away. The food, the weather and the people will be different, the social interaction as well. You have to prepare for this.

    你可能会想家，尤其是当你住得很远的时候。 食物、天气和人都会有所不同，社交方式也不同。你必须为此做好准备。

  • Gather as much info as you can. What is the cost of living : rent, food, transport, hobbies ? You should not only focus on the salary but on the expenses as well. What is the average salary in this city or company ?

  • What is health care like ? That’s a huge topic. To get sick in a foreign country is not fun at all. Ask the studio if they have any private medical care.

    医疗保健怎么样？ 这是一个很大的话题。在国外生病一点也不好玩。问问工作室是否有私人医疗保健。

    公司应该帮助你安顿下来。 如果外国工作室雇佣了你，他们至少应该为你和你的家人提供机票和两周的酒店住宿。这是最低限度的搬迁补助。

  • Take it easy. The beginning of the trip is generally quite stressful : you have to find a place to live, do all the administrative stuff and learn your new job at the same time. Your supervisor should be aware of your situation and act accordingly.

    放轻松。 旅程的开始通常很紧张：你必须找到住处，处理所有行政事务，同时学习新工作。你的主管应该了解你的情况并采取相应措施。

    你的伴侣会怎么做？ 我听过很多情侣在国外分手的故事。这是一次真正的考验。 所以要确保你们双方意见一致。

  • It is not a stupid idea especially if you have kids to travel alone first. It will give you time to settle, find your marks and then you can bring the rest of the family.

    这不是一个愚蠢的想法，特别是如果你有孩子，那么先独自旅行。 这样你会有时间适应，找到自己的目标，然后你就可以带上其他家人。

  • Always leave the door open to come back. If things are not working like you expected, it is okay to go home. This would not be a failure.

    永远留着门可以回来。 如果事情没有像你预期的那样发展，回家也没关系。这不算失败。

The biggest cultural shock I ever experienced was when I moved from Madrid to London. My Spanish colleagues were basically my friends, there is no clear separation between work and private life. Which can be great or not. Ilion was overall a very nice place where people would have lunch together. London on the other hand was very professional, much quieter and a bit cold. I would often eat at my desk. Except at the pub where my colleagues would unexpectedly become different people. I honestly cannot say one model is better than the other.

我经历过的最大文化冲击是从马德里搬到伦敦的时候。我的西班牙同事基本上都是我的朋友，工作和私人生活没有明确的界限。这可能很好，也可能不好。总的来说，Ilion 是一个非常好的地方，人们可以一起吃午饭。另一方面，伦敦非常专业，安静得多，有点冷。我经常在办公桌上吃饭。除了在酒吧，我的同事会意外地变成不同的人。老实说，我不能说一种模式比另一种更好。

They are just different and you should get the best of it.

它们只是不同而已，你应该充分利用它们。

找到你的位置 Find your place

I got this great piece of advice on my first gig, ​Planet 51​. I remember that as a mid lighting artist, I wanted to do lots of shots. That was my goal. To be fast and efficient and render as many shots as possible.

我在第一次演出Planet 51时得到了一条很好的建议。我记得作为一名中级灯光师，我想拍摄很多镜头。那是我的目标。快速高效地渲染尽可能多的镜头。

Another mid lighter on the show chose a completely different approach. He was slower than me but he was getting these great money shots. He was taking his time to work on these amazing lighting setups.

节目中另一位中级灯光师选择了完全不同的方法。他比我慢，但他拍出了这些精彩的照片。他花时间研究这些令人惊叹的灯光设置。

A senior friend of mine then gave me this advice :

我的一位资深朋友给了我这样的建议：

Watch out. Do you want to be categorized as the quantity guy ? If you output many shots quickly, people will get used to it and put a label on you. Ideally which label would you like to have ?

注意。你想被归类为数量型的人吗？如果你快速输出大量镜头，人们会习惯并给你贴上标签。理想情况下，你想被贴上什么标签？

That’s why I don’t code. I have worked for 15 years without doing a single line of mel or python because I don’t want to have a programming label on me. On every show I have worked on, there were so great developers who would take care of it anyway.

这就是我不写代码的原因。我工作了 15 年，没有写过一行 mel 或 python，因为我不想被贴上编程的标签。在我参与的每个节目中，都有很棒的开发人员愿意处理这件事。

But it does not mean that you should not look into regular expressions or scripts. On ​Lego Batman​, our lighting workflow would be based on these amazing scripts called RSS. And this is the closest I ever went to scripting.

但这并不意味着你不应该研究正则表达式或脚本。在 《乐高蝙蝠侠》 中，我们的照明工作流程将基于这些名为 RSS 的惊人脚本。这是我最接近编写脚本的一次。

The Render Submission Script (RSS) from Animal. I do believe that text can be a lifesaver.

Animal 的渲染提交脚本 (RSS)。我相信文本可以成为救星。

We had a RSS template for the whole movie to start from a proper base. And a prefill command to help us typing the correct nomenclature.

我们为整部电影准备了一个 RSS 模板，可以从正确的基础开始。还有一个预填充命令来帮助我们输入正确的术语。

I have had a quantity label on many shows and I was honestly fine with it. Until I got a bit tired of outputting many shots and eventually moved to a lead position.

我在很多节目中都贴过数量标签，说实话我对此很满意。直到我有点厌倦输出大量镜头，最终转向了主角的位置。

对学生和初级艺术家的建议 Advice to students and junior artists

态度 Attitude

As a supervisor, I don’t want to micro-manage nor open your scenes. You are the owner of your shot and are responsible for it. You and only you can make it look good. I hate when artists are just waiting and whining about their shots : “​it doesn’t look good, it is not working. ​” What the heck “​it is not working**​ ” means ?

作为主管，我不想事无巨细地管理或公开你的场景。你是镜头的主人，要对镜头负责。 只有你才能让镜头看起来不错。我讨厌艺术家们只是在等待，抱怨他们的镜头：“看起来不太好，没有效果。 ” “没有效果​ ”到底是什么意思？

Something they probably don’t tell you in school : production is tough and lighting can be a pain in the arse.

他们在学校可能不会告诉你一些事情：制作很困难， 灯光也很麻烦。

  • Why didn’t they approve my shot ? I am working on version 086 !

    他们为什么不批准我的镜头？我正在制作 086 版！

  • Why do I have to re-render for the 7th time ?

    为什么我必须第七次重新渲染？

  • This character was supposed to be fixed in the previous version ! Why is it still broken ?

    这个角色应该在之前的版本中被修复了！为什么它还是坏了？

Sequences get cut, tools don’t work, artistic direction changes several times : that’s part of the job. End of production is always the most difficult moment : people are tired, they make more mistakes and there is somehow less stuff to do.

片段被删减，工具无法使用，艺术指导多次更改：这是工作的一部分。 制作结束总是最困难的时刻：人们很累，犯的错误更多，而且不知何故要做的事情更少了。

As a lead, I am not necessarily looking for experienced people : I have worked with great junior artists. I am looking for attitude and a proactive behavior. It is okay to fail. We all have flaws and we all make mistakes.

作为领队，我不一定寻找经验丰富的人：我曾与优秀的初级艺术家合作过。我寻找的是态度和积极主动的行为。 失败是可以的。我们都有缺点，都会犯错。

But what makes a huge difference in my opinion is your attitude. In case of an error :

但我认为，你的态度才是最重要的。如果出现错误：

  • Don’t panic.

    不要恐慌。

  • No need to bury your head in the sand.

    没有必要把头埋在沙子里。

  • Don’t blame other departments.

    不要责怪其他部门。

  • Tell your lead and other teammates.

    告诉你的领导和其他队友。

  • Look for a solution.

    寻找解决方案。

If you see a mistake or an issue, share it with the team. Do not keep it to yourself. We probably want to fix errors for the entire sequence, not only on your shot. There is always a reward in doing things properly. Believe me. I know this since I make mistakes every day.

如果您发现错误或问题，请与团队分享。 不要自己保留。我们可能想修复整个序列的错误，而不仅仅是您的镜头。正确做事总会有回报。相信我。我知道这一点，因为我每天都会犯错。

检查、再检查、再检查 Check, double-check, triple-check

Double-check your information before sharing with anyone. Too often, I see artists sending e-mails before checking properly their scene. That is the most common mistake among artists. It also compulsory to check what you publish. It will be a great help for everybody.

在与任何人分享之前，请仔细检查您的信息。 我经常看到艺术家在正确检查他们的场景之前发送电子邮件。这是艺术家中最常见的错误。检查您发布的内容也是强制性的。 这对每个人都有很大帮助。

Here is an example from a real production :

以下是真实制作的一个例子：

  • Junior artist : “​Hey, my character’s fur is not rendering. Can you fix it ? ​”

    初级艺术家：“嘿，我的角色的毛发没有渲染。你能修复它吗？ ”

  • Lead : “​Hum, I don’t know. Have you tried deleting and re-importing it ? ​”

    领导：“嗯，我不知道。你试过删除然后重新导入吗？ ”

  • Junior artist : “​No. ​”

    青年艺术家：“​不​。”

First thing to do if an asset is not rendering or broken, import it from scratch in a new scene to test it. Is it still causing an issue ? Have you actually tried a couple of things before asking your lead ? Use dichotomy when you have an issue until your narrow it down :

如果资产无法渲染或损坏，首先要做的是将其从头导入到新场景中进行测试。 它是否仍然会引起问题？在询问领导之前，您是否尝试过几件事？遇到问题时使用二分法，直到缩小范围：

  • Does my render still crash if I remove displacement ?

    如果我删除位移，渲染是否仍会崩溃？

  • What if I remove the characters ?

    如果我删除这些字符会怎么样？

  • Is there a log to check ? Any error message ?

    有日志可以检查吗？有任何错误信息吗？

  • Can you do a very simple scene for TDs to have a look (by turning some lights off or applying a mid gray shader) ?

    您可以为 TD 制作一个非常简单的场景吗（通过关闭一些灯或应用中灰色着色器）？

  • Make it obvious to others. I always do a print screen of the issue and circle it in red. Trust me, this is a huge time saver.

    让其他人一目了然。 我总是截取问题的屏幕截图并用红色圈出。 相信我，这可以节省大量时间。

Reach out to the senior artists. Graduate or junior can learn a lot if they are not afraid to ask. Some companies have mentor ship program where they allocate a senior artist to the junior/graduate.

与资深艺术家取得联系。毕业生或初级艺术家如果不怕提问，可以学到很多东西。一些公司有导师计划，他们会为初级艺术家/毕业生分配一名资深艺术家。

And that’s a great thing !

这是一件很棒的事！

报告问题 Report an issue

If after all these tests, the issue is still not fixed. Maybe you could do a ticket where you explain as best as you can your issue. And be fucking polite. I have seen the following email from a real experience :

如果经过所有这些测试，问题仍未解决。也许你可以写一张票，尽可能详细地解释你的问题。而且要有礼貌。 我见过以下来自真实经历的电子邮件：

  • Junior artist : “​My scene is broken. Fix it. ​”

    初级艺术家：“我的场景坏了。修复它。 ”

  • TD : “​Please ? ​”

    TD：“可以吗？ ”

Start you email with Hello and finish it with ​Thanks​. And cc’ your lead when you report an issue. Simple as that. These small recommendations for communication will be of great help in crunch time.

电子邮件以 “您好”开头，以“谢谢” 结尾。报告问题时请抄送您的领导。就这么简单。这些沟通小建议在关键时刻会大有帮助。

We are all impostors but what really matters is to acknowledge it. As for the rest, it is the collective and the respect that make us move forward.

我们都是冒名顶替者，但真正重要的是承认这一点。至于其他，正是集体和尊重让我们继续前进。

Great advice by Cédric Lejeune.

Cédric Lejeune 的建议非常棒。

说服同事和主管 Convincing colleagues and supervisors

You may reach a point in your career where you’d like to convince your superiors of “something”. This process can be very tiring and frustrating. So here are few tips :

在你的职业生涯中，你可能会达到一个阶段，你想说服你的上司“某事”。这个过程可能非常累人，令人沮丧。所以这里有一些建议：

  • To convince an “expert”, you need real data. Convincing cannot be based on an intuition or a gut feeling, even if you think it’s genius !

    要说服“专家”，你需要真实的数据。 说服不能基于直觉或直觉，即使你认为这是天才！

  • I generally recommand to prepare some slides, adapted to your audience, with proper images to illustrate. Your presentation should be as unambiguous as possible, to hammer very point with clarity.

    我通常建议准备一些适合你的听众的幻灯片，并配上适当的图片来说明。你的演讲应该尽可能清晰，清晰地表达每一个要点。

  • In general, it is very hard to convince people . So it is maybe better to see it as a “conversation” : expose the current situation and focus on the solutions and improvements your approach will bring. There is nothing as strong and convinving than the “soft power”.

    一般来说，说服别人是非常困难的。所以最好把它看作是一次“对话”：揭露当前的情况，并专注于你的方法将带来的解决方案和改进。没有什么比“软实力”更强大、更有说服力了。

Theory offers guidelines, Experiment offers decisions.

理论提供指导，实验提供决策。

This is so true. In CG, theory is not enough.

确实如此。在 CG 领域，光有理论是不够的。

给主管和领导的建议 Advice to supervisors and leads

目的 Purpose

In a perfect world a supervisor should share his vision and explain his philosophy to his crew. I have been for a long time wrong about the supervisor’s role. I have thought for many years that a supervisor should the best lighter on the team. That is so untrue. A supervisor is the one who best manages talent. Which is a very different thing.

在理想的世界中，主管应该与他的团队分享他的愿景并解释他的理念。长期以来，我对主管角色的看法都是错误的。多年来，我一直认为主管应该是团队中最好的人。这太不对了。 主管才是管理人才的最佳人选。这是完全不同的事情。

So each lighter can reach his best potential without any limit to his potential. A supervisor’s role is to establish a trust relationship with his team and set a positive dynamic. He should be able to talk to artists, reassure them, listen to their issues and fix them on a department level.

因此，每个灯光师都可以发挥出自己的最大潜力，没有任何限制。 主管的职责是与团队建立信任关系，并营造积极的氛围。他应该能够与艺术家交谈，安慰他们，倾听他们的问题，并在部门层面解决问题。

We must not underestimate all this human and psychological aspect of a production. It’s the best way to cut rumors, complaints and concerns. The goal is to find the right balance so that everyone can flourish in a defined framework.

我们绝不能低估制作过程中所有这些人性和心理因素。这是消除谣言、抱怨和担忧的最佳方式。目标是找到正确的平衡，以便每个人都能在既定的框架内蓬勃发展。

For example each artist could make a presentation on a subject close to his heart : painting, DOF, cinematography… So that everyone feels involved in the process and in order to generate emulation between the artists. It would be beneficial for everyone.

例如，每位艺术家都可以就自己感兴趣的主题进行演示：绘画、景深、电影摄影……这样每个人都会觉得自己参与到了这个过程当中，从而在艺术家之间产生模仿。这对每个人都有好处。

What I would like to achieve as a supervisor ? If I am away, the team should work the same. You guys know what to do when I am not around. Basically my purpose is to be transparent.

作为主管，我希望实现什么目标？如果我不在，团队应该以同样的方式工作。 你们知道我不在时该做什么。基本上我的目的是透明。

  • A supervisor works on a movie.

    一位监督正在制作一部电影。

  • A lead works on a sequence level.

    线索在序列级别上起作用。

  • An artist works on shots.

    一位艺术家正在创作照片。

I always say : “A good supervisor shows how it works. A bad supervisor says how it works.”

我总是说： “一个好的主管会向人们展示如何运作。一个坏的主管会说如何运作。”

训练 Training

This should be a very important aspect of production but unfortunately not many companies give it sufficient attention. Training is not a ‘bonus’, it should be the base.

这应该是生产中非常重要的一个方面，但不幸的是，许多公司并没有给予足够的重视。培训不是“奖励”，而应该是基础。

At Animal, we had one hour of meeting and training per week. And it was compulsory for anyone from the lighting department.

在 Animal，我们每周有一小时的会议和培训。灯光部门的每个人都必须参加。

Another great idea from Craig.

克雷格的另一个好主意。

  • Training scene for new starters with help from the lead. It is very important that they try out the whole pipeline : build, publish, render a shot. All of this should happened in a separated branch of pipeline to not affect production.

    在主管的帮助下为新人提供培训场景。让他们尝试整个流程非常重要：构建、发布、渲染镜头。所有这些都应该在流程的独立分支中进行，以免影响生产。

  • Homogenization of lighting techniques : the whole idea behind this training is to make people work in a more similar way. If people shares a certain workflow, it will give us more predictable results and it is easier to debug too.

    照明技术的同质化： 这种培训背后的整个想法是让人们以更相似的方式工作。如果人们共享某种工作流程，它将为我们提供更可预测的结果，并且调试起来也更容易。

  • Inter department training : surfacing to lighting, DMP to lighting, art department to lighting… We have to work very closely together to make the best movie possible.

    跨部门培训： 从平面设计到灯光、从 DMP 到灯光、从艺术部门到灯光......我们必须密切合作，才能制作出最好的电影。

  • Training from people outside of the studio : director of photography, director, screenwriter… At Blue Sky, they had a master class by an awesome DP (I think it was Dan Lemmon’s brother) about filming penguins in Antarctica and which kind of lenses they used. Inspiring !

    来自工作室外人员的培训： 摄影指导、导演、编剧……在蓝天工作室，他们举办了一场大师班，由一位出色的摄影指导（我认为他是Dan Lemmon 的兄弟）讲授如何在南极洲拍摄企鹅以及他们使用了哪种镜头。鼓舞人心！

  • Lighting Wiki is also a very important in this process. It has to be updated frequently by leads and supervisors. Otherwise, it is pointless. We used this one at CinesiteFA : Confluence. You can also find some wikis for free like WordPress and some documentation guidelines here.

    在这个过程中，灯光 Wiki也非常重要。它必须由领导和主管经常更新。否则，它就毫无意义了。我们在 CinesiteFA 使用了Confluence。您还可以在这里找到一些免费的 Wiki，例如WordPress和一些文档指南。

Too much often, unfortunately, companies see training as a loss of money since we are not producing anything. But I am 100% convinced that on the long-term you make better movies and cheaper too.

不幸的是，很多时候公司认为培训是一种损失，因为我们什么都没做。但我百分之百地相信，从长远来看，你可以制作出更好的电影，而且成本更低。

部门生活 Life in the department

It is also quite crucial to keep the spirits high during production. A movie production can be tough and frustrating. It is vital that people can express these feelings freely. Here is a list of ideas on how to manage those :

在制作过程中保持高昂的情绪也非常重要。电影制作可能既艰难又令人沮丧。人们能够自由地表达这些感受至关重要。以下是有关如何管理这些情绪的一些想法：

  • Screening of movie on your first day of work and an invitation for lunch. Screening every 6 months to get the artists up-to-date with the movie.

    上班第一天放映电影并邀请共进午餐。每 6 个月放映一次，让艺术家了解电影的最新动态。

  • One hour meeting per week for the department : this meeting is the place where artists can express their concerns or questions. It must be a secure place where people can speak freely. It is also a place where the supervisor givesupdates on the project, where a TD can do a demo of a new tool, screening of a short film or a movie, lighting and painting study…

    部门每周开一小时会议： 这个会议是艺术家表达他们的担忧或疑问的地方。它必须是一个安全的地方，人们可以自由发言。 它也是主管更新项目进度的地方，TD 可以演示新工具，放映短片或电影，进行灯光和绘画研究……

  • Reviews once a day before lunch with the lighting supervisor. Artists work during the afternoon on their notes and asks their leads for help.

    每天午餐前与灯光主管一起回顾一次。下午艺术家们根据笔记进行创作，并向他们的指导老师寻求帮助。

  • Separated dailies : you don’t want to mix the reviews and improvise in front of your team. Morning dailies are for the lighting crew only. Once a week, the lighting supervisor meets with the director to show the shots and catch up with him on the sequences. If the director criticizes shots in front of everybody, it just diminishes the lighting sup’s aura.

    每日分开： 你不想在团队面前混杂评论和即兴发挥。晨间每日只供灯光组使用。每周一次，灯光主管会与导演会面，展示镜头并跟进镜头。如果导演在所有人面前批评镜头，那只会削弱灯光主管的气场。

  • Shot of the month with little prizes for the lighters. It can be voted by the whole department for the most astonishing shot. The winner can explain how he worked on his shot and share with his colleagues his techniques.

    本月最佳镜头， 并为打火机手提供小奖品。整个部门可以投票选出最令人惊艳的镜头。获胜者可以解释他是如何拍摄这张照片的，并与同事分享他的技巧。

  • Once a month : organize a screening of all APP LIGHTING shots with beers offered by production.

    每月一次： 组织放映所有 APP LIGHTING 镜头以及制作方提供的啤酒。

  • Once a month : Show your stuff ! Screening of assets and shots from different departments with a presentation and an update about the project… And beers !

    每月一次： 展示您的作品！放映来自不同部门的资产和镜头，并进行演示和项目更新……还有啤酒！

团队建设 Team building

  • Party celebration for 25%, 50%, 75% and 100% of the movie. So people feel part of the process and celebrate their hard work.

    电影放映 25%、50%、75% 和 100% 时，都会举行派对庆祝。 这样人们就会觉得自己是整个过程的一部分，并庆祝自己的辛勤工作。

  • Cine club, drawing class to watch movies and train your artists.

    电影俱乐部、绘画课， 观看电影并培养您的艺术家。

  • T-Shirts at the end of production.

    生产完毕的T 恤 。

  • Training camp : Put everyone in the projection room, take a few shots (good and bad) and have a proper demo on the video projector.

    训练营： 把每个人都放在放映室里，拍一些镜头（好的和坏的），并在视频投影仪上进行适当的演示。

  • Forget my shot mini-bottles when shots are Out Of Picture (OOP).

    当镜头超出画面范围（OOP）时，忘记我的镜头迷你瓶。

  • Work outing once in a while (bowling, paintball…) also help for team building.

    偶尔进行锻炼（保龄球、彩弹射击……）也有助于团队建设。

  • Breakfast with the director every 3 months. Allows artists and the director to chat around a warm coffee about the movie.

    每三个月与导演共进早餐。让艺术家和导演围着热咖啡聊聊电影。

  • Get them involved : it is also crucial that motivation stays high during production. Everyone must feel like working at his best potential. Smaller teams give more cohesion.

    让他们参与进来： 在生产过程中保持高昂的积极性也很重要。每个人都必须觉得自己在发挥自己的最大潜力。小团队更有凝聚力。

  • Team, team, team : If teams are stable, it gives more cohesion to the group. Depending on inventory, you can add 1 or 2 persons. Small teams give better results.

    团队、团队、团队： 如果团队稳定，团队的凝聚力就会更强。根据库存，您可以增加 1 或 2 人。小团队效果更好。

  • Chat rooms : The best way I have seen to keep everyone informed. A chat with the whole department where you canshare informationslike “​Latest toolchain is broken. Don’t use it​“. Because ​you don’t want to receive 200 emails per day​, chat is very handy.

    聊天室： 我见过的让每个人都了解情况的最佳方式。与整个部门聊天，您可以分享信息， 例如“最新工具链坏了。不要使用它”。因为你不想每天收到 200 封电子邮件，所以聊天非常方便。

团队管理 Team management

I am no expert in management as I have learned it the hard way. But here are a few tips based on my personal experience. I write these recommendations for myself as well to keep me on track. Because a friendly reminder never hurts :

我不是管理方面的专家，因为我学得很辛苦。但这里有一些基于我个人经验的建议。我也为自己写了这些建议，以保持我的正轨。因为一个友好的提醒永远不会有坏处：

  • Be cool. Be confident. Don’t try to please people.
  • 保持冷静。保持自信。不要试图取悦别人。
  • Let them work as they want. A team is about trust. Don’t be a control freak.
  • 让他们按自己想要的方式工作。团队就是信任。 不要成为控制狂。
  • Don’t take the work from your lighters when they are away for lunch or at night.
  • 午餐或晚上外出时，请勿使用打火机进行工作。
  • Make their life simpler. Don’t over complicate things and share the load.
  • 让他们的生活更简单。不要让事情变得过于复杂，分担负担。
  • Acknowledge the elephant in the room. If things go wrong, speak up !
  • 承认房间里的大象。 如果事情出错，大声说出来！

Think about the leads you had as an artist. Think about what you liked or disliked about them. As a lead, you now have the official power to organize a meeting.

想想你作为艺术家所领导的团队。想想你喜欢或不喜欢他们什么。作为领导，你现在有组织会议的正式权力。

You can book a room with a projector to show references, make a presentation about lighting or movies. That’s awesome ! Don’t copy the leads you had. Be the lead you always dreamed to have.

您可以预订一个带投影仪的房间来展示参考资料，进行有关灯光或电影的演示。太棒了！不要抄袭您已有的线索。成为您一直梦想拥有的线索。

Here are my personal recommendations :

以下是我的个人建议：

  • SPEAK. Don’t keep things inside as you will eventually explode at some point.
  • 说出来。 别把事情憋在心里，因为你最终会在某个时候爆发。
  • Your artists should feel responsible for their shots and involved in the process.
  • 你的艺术家应该对他们的镜头负责并参与到整个过程中。
  • Workload should be shared fairly among the team.
  • 工作量应在团队之间公平分配。
  • Ask them : Do you need help ? Can you fix it ? Are you okay with this ? Don’t ever say, especially in the heat of the moment : ​I told you not to do so​.
  • 问他们： 你需要帮助吗？你能修好它吗？你同意吗？永远不要说，特别是在情绪激动的时候：​我告诉过你不要这么做​。

艺人管理 Artist management

Most of the time lighting artists want two things :

大多数时候，灯光艺术家想要两件事：

  • Interesting shots.
  • 有趣的镜头。
  • To work at ease (aka to be left in peace).
  • 安心工作（即安静地工作）。

The most complicated part about being a lead is “shot assignation”. Because people compare their shots, speak about them and eventually they can be jealous. Sometimes you have a junior who is more efficient than a senior. So who are you going to give the good shots to ? Anyway most important is to :

担任主管最复杂的部分是“镜头分配”。因为人们会比较他们的镜头，谈论他们，最终他们会嫉妒。有时你的初级人员比高级人员更有效率。那么你要把好的镜头交给谁呢？无论如何，最重要的是：

  • Trust them.
  • 相信他们。
  • Check with them from time to time.
  • 时不时地向他们核实一下。
  • Let them manage their shit.
  • 让他们自己处理他们的事吧。

An important point is to anticipate as much as you can the sequences. So you are always a step ahead of the team, like a mountain guide. A team is about trust, it is like building a living organism.

一个重要的点是尽可能多地预测事件的顺序。这样你就能像登山向导一样，始终领先团队一步。团队就是信任，就像构建一个活的有机体。

Finally people will remember the good moments aside of work. Have lunch with them, a drink or go to the movies. For example, if you have a whiskey bottle on your desk with some glasses. When morale is low, have a shot with them and let’s go back to it ! It seems stupid but people will remember this kind of details. It helps to set a good atmosphere.

最后，人们会记住工作之外的美好时刻。与他们共进午餐、喝一杯或看电影。例如，如果你的桌子上有一瓶威士忌和一些玻璃杯。当士气低落时，和他们喝一杯，然后我们再回去吧！这看起来很愚蠢，但人们会记住这种细节。它有助于营造良好的氛围。

结论 Conclusion

Well this was my list of advice from an old fool. I hope I did not scare you too much with all my stories. But I really think that we should share our experience rather than keeping it from students. Finally here is a great proverb to help you get prepared : “Hope for the best, prepare for the worst.“

好吧，这是我从一位老傻瓜那里得到的建议清单。我希望我的故事没有吓到你。但我真的认为我们应该分享我们的经验，而不是对学生隐瞒。最后，这里有一句很棒的谚语可以帮助你做好准备： “抱最好的希望，做最坏的打算。”
